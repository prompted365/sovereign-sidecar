# Council Resonance

> Reference for the `Council Resonance` column in the quiver table (`CLAUDE.md` →
> Quiver). This explains *why* each governance arrow resonates with the council
> plates it names.

## What the councils are

The **40 Councils of Ubiquity** are not committees — they are **tension plates**:
historically validated polarity fields (e.g. *Utilitarian Calculus vs Virtue Ethics*,
*Memetic Warfare vs Cultural Dialogue*) that generate the pressures any governance
system must hold. They are a construct of the full [Ubiquity](https://promptedllc.com)
substrate; the sidecar borrows only the handful its arrows touch.

An arrow **resonates** with a plate when the arrow's stance actively holds that
plate's tension — when firing the arrow nudges a situation toward the healthy pole of
that polarity. Resonance is design rationale, not runtime behavior: the router never
reads this file. It explains what each arrow is *for*, in the field's own terms.

The full 40-plate field, and the mapping of federation invariants onto it, lives in
Ubiquity (`autonomous_kernel/invariant-council-mapping.md`). This is the sidecar's
local slice.

## /ingest — metabolize (#9, #20, #40)

*Fires before external context enters. Returns shaped context. Stance: metabolize, don't cargo-cult.*

- **#9 Techno-Solutionism vs Wisdom-Guided Innovation** — `/ingest` refuses to adopt
  an outside tool, frame, or pattern just because it exists. It metabolizes the
  external thing against *purpose* before letting it in. That is wisdom guiding what
  technology enters, not solutionism importing it wholesale.
- **#20 Academic Knowledge vs Indigenous Epistemologies** — external context is treated
  as *one* way of knowing, integrated on its merits, never as an authority that must be
  copied verbatim. `/ingest` holds the plate that says credentialed external form does
  not outrank in-context knowing.
- **#40 Academic Credentialism vs Experiential Knowledge** — `/ingest` asks whether the
  imported thing *works here*, not whether its source is prestigious. Cargo-culting is
  credentialism; metabolization is experiential validation.

## /tom — centroid-preserving re-expression (#14, #18, #22)

*Fires before expression. Returns re-expressed content. Stance: preserve the centroid across the seam.*

- **#14 Memetic Warfare vs Cultural Dialogue** — `/tom` re-expresses to **preserve**
  meaning across audiences. That is the exact inverse of memetic distortion, which
  reshapes meaning to spread. Holding the centroid is choosing dialogue over contagion.
- **#18 Linguistic Imperialism vs Multilingual Dignity** — `/tom` translates *into* the
  receiver's frame rather than imposing the author's. Re-expression that respects the
  audience's language is multilingual dignity; demanding the audience decode your frame
  is the imperial pole.
- **#22 Artificial Intelligence vs Augmented Wisdom** — `/tom` uses the model to *carry*
  human intent faithfully, not to replace it. Centroid-preservation is augmentation: the
  meaning the human holds survives the machine's re-expression.

## /complement — cooperative-topological closure (#3, #6)

*Fires after a move lands. Returns the surfaces the move exposed.*

- **#3 Utilitarian Calculus vs Virtue Ethics** — `/complement` looks past the local
  metric win to what the move *exposed* elsewhere. A purely utilitarian view stops at the
  scored outcome; the complement pass cares about the whole field the move touched. That
  is the virtue pole — judgment of the whole, not optimization of the part.
- **#6 Radical Individualism vs Relational Selfhood** — closure is a *relational*
  property: a move's completeness is defined by its effect on neighboring surfaces, not by
  the move in isolation. `/complement` reads the relations, holding the selfhood-is-relational pole.

## /counter — adversarial falsification (#1, #12, #23)

*Fires before a move commits. Returns the strongest case against.*

- **#1 Machiavellian Power vs Moral Authority** — `/counter` is the gate that stops a
  power-move from committing unexamined. Moral authority earns its action by surviving the
  strongest case against it; raw power skips that step.
- **#12 Algorithmic Governance vs Human Wisdom** — `/counter` injects deliberate
  adversarial judgment *before* an automatic or algorithmic commit. The falsification pass
  is the human-wisdom pole resisting frictionless execution.
- **#23 Game Theory vs Cooperative Strategy** — `/counter` attacks the idea to
  **strengthen** it, not to win against its author. Cooperative adversariality: the
  strongest-case-against serves the shared outcome, which is the cooperative pole.

## Procedural arrows — no primary plate (`/citation-intel`, `/posture`)

`/citation-intel` (publication readiness) and `/posture` (working-mode contract) carry
**no primary council resonance**, marked `—` in the quiver. They are *procedural* arrows:
they enforce process integrity — extractability, token discipline, declared operating
mode — rather than holding a values tension across a polarity field. Their value is
discipline, not resonance. (Should a future arrow or refinement reveal a plate either one
genuinely holds, add it here and update the quiver table together.)

## The upgrade lane

In the sidecar, resonance is documentation — a way to see what each arrow is for. In the
full [Ubiquity](https://promptedllc.com) substrate, the same 40-plate field is *live*:
positions are tracked, trajectories move across the field, and governance is field
stabilization rather than rule-following. If you find yourself wanting to know *where you
are* in the field rather than just *what an arrow is for*, that is the substrate calling.
